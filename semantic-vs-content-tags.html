Comparison

Both versions look identical, but they are not equally usable. Screen readers can announce landmarks like header, nav, main, and footer, which lets someone using assistive technology jump straight to the article body instead of listening to every line of navigation and boilerplate first. A div gives none of that information; it is invisible to a screen reader's landmark list because it has no meaning of its own.

The same is true for strong and em versus b and i. strong and em tell assistive technology that text carries real importance or emphasis, which can change how it is read aloud, while b and i only change how the text looks on screen and carry no meaning at all for someone who cannot see it.

Search engines and other machines benefit the same way people using screen readers do: an article tag tells a search engine "this block is the actual content," which is more useful for indexing and ranking than a page built entirely from generic div containers with no identity of their own.
